# Email Follow-Up Sequences

Use these sequences after the first contact, proposal, or meeting. Always respect `Do Not Contact` and consent flags.

## Sequence A: New enquiry — no response

### Day 1 — Personal first response

- **Goal:** Acknowledge, add value, propose a next step.
- **Subject:** Re: [ORIGINAL_SUBJECT]
- **Body:** See `email-templates/personal-first-response.md`.

### Day 4 — Check-in

- **Goal:** Stay top of mind without being pushy.
- **Subject:** Quick follow-up — [CUSTOMER_NAME]
- **Body:** Short, friendly check-in. Offer a resource or case study.

### Day 8 — Add value

- **Goal:** Provide useful insight related to their need.
- **Subject:** Something that might help with [TOPIC]
- **Body:** Share a relevant article, case study, or observation.

### Day 14 — Final polite follow-up

- **Goal:** Close the loop gracefully.
- **Subject:** Should I close the loop?
- **Body:** See `email-templates/final-polite-follow-up.md`.

## Sequence B: After proposal sent

### Day 0 — Proposal sent

- **Goal:** Confirm receipt and outline next steps.
- **Subject:** Your [SERVICE] proposal from Luxival
- **Body:** See `email-templates/proposal-sent.md`.

### Day 3 — Proposal follow-up

- **Goal:** Answer questions and move towards a decision.
- **Subject:** Questions about your proposal?
- **Body:** See `email-templates/proposal-follow-up.md`.

### Day 7 — Objection response or value reinforcement

- **Goal:** Address any unspoken concerns.
- **Subject:** One thing I forgot to mention
- **Body:** Reinforce key benefit and offer a call.

### Day 12 — Final follow-up

- **Goal:** Get a yes/no decision.
- **Subject:** Should we move forward or park this?
- **Body:** See `email-templates/final-polite-follow-up.md`.

## Sequence C: After meeting

### Within 2 hours — Thank you / recap

- **Goal:** Confirm understanding and agreed actions.
- **Subject:** Thank you — next steps from our call
- **Body:** Recap points, list action items, propose next meeting.

### Day 2 — Send promised materials

- **Goal:** Deliver what was promised.
- **Subject:** As promised: [MATERIAL]
- **Body:** Attach or link materials.

### Day 5 — Decision follow-up

- **Goal:** Move to proposal or next meeting.
- **Subject:** Quick question about our next step
- **Body:** Friendly prompt.

## Sequence D: Lost lead — nurture

### Month 1 — Stay in touch

- **Goal:** Remain helpful without selling.
- **Subject:** [Relevant industry insight]
- **Body:** Share useful content.

### Month 3 — Re-engage

- **Goal:** Check if circumstances changed.
- **Subject:** Catching up — [CUSTOMER_NAME]
- **Body:** Short, personalised re-engagement.

### Month 6 — Final loop or unsubscribe

- **Goal:** Give an easy opt-out.
- **Subject:** One last note
- **Body:** "If this is no longer relevant, just reply STOP."

## Rules for all sequences

- Stop the sequence if the lead replies.
- Stop if `Do Not Contact` is TRUE.
- Adapt tone to the prospect's language (English/Finnish).
- Track every email in the **Interactions** sheet.
- Limit total follow-ups to 4–5 before moving to long-term nurture.
