<h1>Agentic workflow playbooks for modern software testers</h1>

<div class="post-body">
  <p>Great QA is more than test scripts. A modern software tester needs a workflow that blends human judgment with reliable automation. At Luxival, we frame <strong>agentic workflow</strong> design as a layered system: agents assist with data gathering, coverage checks, and reporting, while humans own prioritisation and release confidence.</p>

  <p>This keeps delivery fast without sacrificing accountability. You still control the go/no-go decision, but your agents surface the evidence, risks, and next-best actions ahead of each release.</p>

  <h2>Three layers we use in practice</h2>
  <ul>
    <li><strong>Signal layer</strong>: agents collect logs, test results, and regression deltas across environments.</li>
    <li><strong>Decision layer</strong>: QA leads validate the findings, map risks, and set the release posture.</li>
    <li><strong>Action layer</strong>: agents prepare tickets, draft bug reports, and update traceability docs.</li>
  </ul>

  <p>When these layers are clear, automation feels trustworthy and the QA team stays in control. This is the core of a resilient agentic workflow—built for teams who treat quality as a business asset.</p>
