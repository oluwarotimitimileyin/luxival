<h1>SEO for Finnish Businesses: How to Rank on Google in Helsinki</h1>

<div class="post-body">
  <p>Google is the first place most people in Finland look when they need a local service. Whether they're searching for a restaurant in Tampere, a plumber in Espoo, or a chauffeur service in Helsinki — the businesses that appear at the top of those results get the calls. The ones on page two rarely do.</p>

  <p>Local SEO is not complicated, but it requires consistency. Here's where Finnish businesses should focus their effort.</p>

  <h2>Google Business Profile: Your Most Important Free Tool</h2>
  <p>If you serve local customers and you don't have a verified Google Business Profile, you're invisible in map searches. Set it up, fill in every field, add real photos, and collect reviews from genuine customers. A complete, active Business Profile is the single highest-impact action a local Finnish business can take for online visibility.</p>

  <h2>Write in the Language Your Customers Search In</h2>
  <p>Many Finnish business websites are well written in Finnish — but their international customers search in English. If you serve tourists, expats, or international clients, your website needs English content that targets the keywords those customers actually use. "Helsinki airport taxi" gets far more English-language searches than any Finnish equivalent.</p>

  <h2>Target Location + Service Keywords</h2>
  <p>Generic keywords are highly competitive and expensive to rank for. Local keywords are not. "Web design Helsinki", "airport transfer Vantaa", "sauna restaurant Tampere" — these longer, location-specific phrases have less competition and much higher buying intent. Build individual pages or blog posts around each one.</p>

  <h2>Backlinks Still Matter</h2>
  <p>A backlink is a link from another website pointing to yours. Google treats backlinks as votes of trust. Finnish business directories, local press coverage, guest posts on industry blogs, and partnerships with complementary businesses are all legitimate ways to build them. The blog post you're reading right now is an example of how content can earn those links.</p>

  <h2>Speed and Mobile Experience</h2>
  <p>Google uses page speed and mobile-friendliness as ranking factors. A slow website won't rank well, regardless of how good the content is. If your site scores below 60 on Google's PageSpeed Insights for mobile, that's a problem worth fixing before anything else.</p>
