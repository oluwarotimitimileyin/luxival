Website Maintenance Services

A website that launches and is never touched again will eventually develop problems: broken links, outdated content, compatibility issues after browser updates, and forms that stop working. Luxival provides practical maintenance support to keep your site in working order.

Problems This Service Solves

  • Content is outdated and the team has no straightforward way to update it.
  • Links on the site have broken but nobody knows where or how many.
  • Forms work inconsistently after a recent browser or dependency update.
  • The site displays incorrectly on newer devices or screen sizes.
  • Small bugs accumulate and degrade the user experience over time.
  • No one is reviewing the site periodically to catch issues before they become visible to visitors.

What You Get

  • Content updates: text changes, new sections, and page edits as needed.
  • Bug fixes for layout issues, broken interactions, and JavaScript errors.
  • Broken-link checks to identify and fix dead internal and external links.
  • Compatibility checks after browser updates or dependency changes.
  • Form testing to confirm all contact and booking forms are submitting correctly.
  • Responsive checks to validate the site still works on newer device sizes.
  • Ongoing QA on key user flows to catch regressions early.
  • Technical maintenance to address specific issues as they arise.

Working Process

  1. Initial review: check the site for existing issues before starting regular support.
  2. Priority fix: address any critical problems found in the initial review.
  3. Scheduled maintenance: run agreed checks at a regular cadence.
  4. Issue reporting: document findings with reproduction steps and recommended fixes.
  5. Ongoing support: handle updates, fixes, and questions as they come in.

Who This Is Suitable For

  • Businesses that launched a website and need ongoing technical support.
  • Teams without in-house developers who can handle website issues quickly.
  • Companies that want a periodic review of their site's health and content.
  • Organisations that have had bugs accumulate and need a structured catch-up.

FAQ

What does website maintenance include?
Maintenance work can include content updates, bug fixes, broken-link checks, compatibility checks after browser or dependency updates, form testing, responsive checks, and addressing specific technical issues as they arise.

How often should a website be maintained?
Frequency depends on how often the site changes and how critical uptime is for your business. Even stable sites benefit from periodic checks to catch compatibility or link issues.

Can you fix bugs on a site you did not build?
Yes. Maintenance support can apply to existing sites built by other teams, provided the codebase is accessible and in reasonable condition.

Is ongoing QA included?
Yes. Maintenance engagements can include regular QA checks on key user flows to catch regressions before they affect real visitors.

Do you provide continuous monitoring?
Scheduled maintenance checks are included. Continuous automated uptime monitoring is a separate arrangement — contact us to discuss what level of support is appropriate for your site.
